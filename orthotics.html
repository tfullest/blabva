Orthotics

What is an Orthotic?

An Orthotic or Orthosis is a device that supports a body part without replacing it.
We make orthoses for your head, shoulder, knees, toes, wrists, and even your spine! Explore some of the products we make, but please note this is not a comprehensive list. Contact us with any questions about what orthoses we can help your family with, or click here to explore our prosthetics!

  • Ankle Foot Orthoses

    AFOs, also known as Ankle Foot Orthoses, are a type of orthotic device that can serve a variety of functions. Depending on what a person needs, an AFO might help them to improve the position of their foot and ankle, to aid in ambulation, to increase their range of motion, or even sometimes to protect a surgery or injury.

  • Cranial Remolding Orthoses

    Cranial Remolding Orthoses (casually referred to as helmets, but make sure you're specific if you're talking to a doctor!) are sometimes used to help babies grow round heads, especially if there is a flat spot or if they've had a special surgery.

  • Knee Ankle Foot Orthoses

    Knee Ankle Foot Orthoses, also known as KAFOs can help people to walk or stand, protect from injuries, or can influence the range of motion. For example, a KAFO could help someone stop their knee from going in a direction it's not supposed to, or depending on the style it could help them move their knee more in the right direction.

  • Supramalleolar Orthoses

    Supramalleolar Orthoses (SMOs) provide a bit of support to the foot. They can be helpful for people that toe-walk, have down syndrome, or for supplying a bit of support to stop the foot from moving in ways it's not supposed to.

  • Thoraco-Lumbar-Sacral Orthoses

    Thoraco-Lumbar-Sacral Orthoses are called TLSOs. Their name refers to the different parts of the back that the brace touches. These can be used for a variety of reasons. Generally, they help to improve spinal alignment. They can be helpful for reducing scoliosis, lordosis, kyphosis, or to help a patient sit upright if they don't have a lot of muscle control.
