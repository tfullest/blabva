What is an Orthotic?

An Orthotic is a device that improves the function of an existing body part, but doesn't replace a part that's missing.
There are many different types of orthotic devices. You may have heard them called "orthoses", if there were two, "orthosis" if there was only one, or you may have heard of a "foot orthotic" which is a special type of support that usually goes inside a shoe. Another word that is similar to "orthotics" is "prosthetics", which is a medical device that replaces a body part that isn't there. Orthotics and prosthetics exist for nearly every part of the body to aid in a wide variety of functions. (You can learn more about prosthetics here.)
